COMPUTERS

The real breakthrough in mechanical computation came in the 19th century with Charles Babbage. Often called the "father of the computer," Babbage designed the Difference Engine, a mechanical device intended to automate polynomial calculations. Although it was never completed during his lifetime. His later design, the Analytical Engine, introduced the idea of a programmable machine, complete with a central processing unit (CPU) and memory.
